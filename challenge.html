Global Challenge

The integrity of marine and coastal ecosystems is challenged by the consequences of climate changes, such as ocean warming, acidification and oxygen loss; marine pollution by microplastic and harmful chemical waste, as well as unsustainable fishing.

The oceans absorb 23% of CO2 generated by human activity and more than 90% of the excess heat in our climate system. Increase of ocean temperature and generation of heat waves threaten marine ecosystems depending on stable water temperature and are one of the major causes coral reef deaths.

Around 5 to 12 billion metric tonnes of plastic enters the ocean every year. About 89% of plastic waste found on ocean floor are single use items. This costs the world an estimate of $ 13 billion per year, along with the clean-up costs and economical loss in fishing and other industries [Reference 2].

Overfishing together with bycatch, the capture of unwanted sea life while fishing for a different species, is one of the biggest drivers of decrease in marine wildlife populations. It causes not only an imbalance in the marine food chain that are closely tied through the food web, but also threatens the livelihood of over 3 billion people [Reference 3].
